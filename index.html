About the Programme

The online Master of Arts in India Studies offered by the Jindal India Institute (JII) at O.P. Jindal Global University (JGU) gives you a comprehensive understanding of India's complexity and global relevance. Over one year, you will explore India's politics, history, philosophy, economy, civilisation, culture, music, cinema and more — so you gain both depth and breadth of knowledge.

Designed for flexibility, the 100% online format allows you to learn from leading faculty while balancing work and personal commitments. Through immersive coursework and real-world engagements, you will develop a well-rounded perspective on India's diversity and its evolving role in the global landscape, preparing you for careers across academia, policy, business, and beyond.

Case Studies on Culture, Society, & Cinema

A curated set of case studies examining how heritage, migration, and films have shaped India's identity at home and across the world.

Cultural Heritage and Soft Power

This case study looks at how the arts function as a core component of cultural strength and national prosperity. India's rich musical traditions give the country a unique opportunity to project its intangible heritage at home and abroad. The study considers how public-private collaboration, supported by technological innovation, can help India use culture as a source of soft power.

Read More

Ancient Traditions and Nation Building

This case study looks at how India's old music and dance traditions help shape national identity. These traditions are not only forms of entertainment; they carry ideas and values that have been passed down for centuries. By protecting and promoting them, a nation can stay connected to its roots and draw guidance from its past.

Read More

From Past to Present: The Journey of the Indian Diaspora

This case study traces the journey of Indians who moved to different parts of the world. It covers early traders, indentured workers during colonial rule, and today's professionals. The study shows how the diaspora influences the culture, economy, and politics of both India and their new countries.

Read More

Deewar

This case study explores the thrilling Salim-Javed blockbuster hit from the 1970s. It connects the rise of the "Angry Young Man" archetype to the turbulent socio-political context of India during the Emergency period. The narrative allows us to empathize more deeply with the rise and fall of grey protagonists in Bollywood cinema.

Know About JII & M.A. India Studies

The Jindal India
Institute

The Jindal India Institute (JII) is the flagship think-tank initiative of the O.P. Jindal Global University (JGU), aimed at promoting a comprehensive and contextual understanding of India around the world. India's rising influence in the world has created an unprecedented demand for a richer, more textured, and interdisciplinary understanding of its complexities – historical, cultural, political, economic, and philosophical.

JII responds to this demand through a wide range of initiatives, including training programmes for foreign diplomats, research projects, expert-led public events, and a pioneering Master of Arts (M.A.) in India Studies, the first of its kind in the online domain, and the only such programme in India.
